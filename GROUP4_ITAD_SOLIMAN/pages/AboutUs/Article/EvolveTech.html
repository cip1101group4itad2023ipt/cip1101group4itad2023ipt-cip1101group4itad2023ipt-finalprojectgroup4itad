Evolution of Technology



Time Period Technological Advancements
Prehistoric Era 2.6 million years ago - Development of early stone tools
Ancient Civilization 4000 BCE - Invention of writing systems (e.g., cuneiform, hieroglyphics)
3000 BCE - Development of agricultural tools and techniques
2600 BCE - Construction of the Great Pyramid of Giza
Middle Ages 1450 - Invention of the printing press by Johannes Gutenberg
1495 - Leonardo da Vinci designs flying machines
1712 - Invention of the steam engine by Thomas Newcomen
Industrial Revolution 1769 - Invention of the steam-powered spinning machine by James Watt
1804 - First successful steam locomotive by Richard Trevithick
1837 - Invention of the telegraph by Samuel Morse
19th Century 1876 - Alexander Graham Bell patents the telephone
1888 - Introduction of the Kodak camera by George Eastman
1897 - First successful typewriter by Christopher Latham Sholes
20th Century 1903 - Wright brothers' first successful airplane flight
1927 - Invention of television by Philo Farnsworth and Vladimir Zworykin
1936 - First programmable computer, the Z1, created by Konrad Zuse
1947 - Invention of the transistor by John Bardeen, Walter Brattain, and William Shockley
1969 - ARPANET, the precursor to the internet, is established
Information Age 1971 - Introduction of the microprocessor by Intel
1973 - First handheld mobile phone by Martin Cooper
1981 - IBM introduces the personal computer (PC)
1989 - Creation of the World Wide Web by Tim Berners-Lee
1991 - Launch of the first web browser, WorldWideWeb
2004 - Launch of Facebook, the first major social media platform
Present and Future 2007 - Introduction of the iPhone, popularizing smartphones
2008 - Release of the first commercial cloud computing services
2010 - Emergence of blockchain technology with the introduction of Bitcoin
2016 - Introduction of virtual reality (VR) headsets like Oculus Rift
2019 - Commercial rollout of 5G networks
2020 - Advancements in artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning applications
Future - Advancements in quantum computing, biotechnology, autonomous vehicles, and renewable energy technologies
