Biometric Authentication



What is Biometric Authentication?


Biometric authentication is a security process that uses the unique physical characteristics of an individual to verify their identity. Biometric authentication relies on biometric data, which can be obtained through various means such as fingerprint scans, iris scans, facial recognition, and voice recognition. The process of biometric authentication involves capturing biometric data from an individual and comparing it with the biometric data stored in a database. If there is a match, the individual is granted access to a system or service, and if there is no match, access is denied.

Biometric authentication is considered to be more secure than traditional authentication methods such as passwords or PINs, as biometric data is unique to each individual and cannot be easily replicated or stolen. Moreover, biometric authentication is more convenient for users, as they don't have to remember complex passwords or carry around physical tokens such as ID cards or security tokens.Biometric authentication is used in a wide range of applications, including access control systems, mobile devices, and financial services. In the financial industry, biometric authentication is increasingly being used to verify the identity of customers and prevent fraud in transactions.
